EBITDA Margin Calculator

Measure how much of revenue becomes EBITDA: enter total revenue and EBITDA for the same period. You get EBITDA margin as a percent of revenue.

Inputs

Use one currency. EBITDA should match the revenue period (month, quarter, or year).

Top-line revenue for the period.
Earnings before interest, taxes, depreciation, and amortization (can be negative).

Results

EBITDA margin = EBITDA ÷ total revenue × 100 when revenue is greater than zero.

EBITDA margin —

Example

Total revenue is $200,000 and EBITDA is $40,000. EBITDA margin is $40,000 ÷ $200,000 × 100 = 20%.

How it works

EBITDA margin (percent) = EBITDA ÷ total revenue × 100. If revenue is zero, margin is not shown. Negative EBITDA yields a negative margin percentage.

Business tip

Compare EBITDA margin across periods with the same accounting policy—definitions of EBITDA can differ by company.
