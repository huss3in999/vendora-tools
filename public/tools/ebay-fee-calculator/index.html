What this tool does

This calculator reconstructs the full buyer payment for eBay or Etsy sales, then walks each fee line—listing, final value, fixed order charges, promoted listings, offsite ads, payment processing, regulatory extras, and your own cost of goods plus outbound shipping—until it lands on net profit and margin percentage. eBay logic models tiered final value fees on the total amount paid (item + shipping + estimated tax) with different breakpoints for standard versus store subscriptions, plus the small fixed per-order fee. Etsy mode layers per-listing renewal costs, transaction percentages on the order total, regional processing rates, and optional offsite ad surcharges. A waterfall table and stacked bar make it obvious which category eats the most margin so you can adjust price, shipping, or ad spend before you list.

Who should use it

Resellers, handmade sellers, vintage curators, and micro-brands juggling both marketplaces will rely on it whenever they price new inventory or audit a month-end statement. Accountants supporting ecommerce clients can sanity-check exports, while restaurant-adjacent merchants selling merchandise or packaged goods online get the same clarity. Because the tool separates marketplace fees from your true variable costs, it is also helpful when you compare multichannel strategies—knowing net dollars after fees is the only fair way to stack Etsy against eBay against your own Shopify site.

How to use it

Pick the platform tab, then enter item price, quantity, shipping you charge, and sales tax assumptions that mirror how buyers check out. Toggle store status, promoted listing rates, international surcharges, or Etsy-specific options like offsite ads and regulatory fees. Add your product cost and shipping-out cost so profit is real, not just fee math. Hit calculate and read the summary cards first, then expand the waterfall to verify each line item. When policies change, update percentages inside the form and save screenshots for your records—always confirm live rates in seller hubs because marketplaces adjust tiers without notice.

Example

Suppose you sell a $45 handmade apron with $8 buyer shipping and $4 estimated tax, so the buyer pays $57. On eBay without a store, final value fees apply to that full total using the tiered percentages in the tool, then the fixed order fee applies. After subtracting $18 material cost and $6 outbound postage, you might see $22 net and roughly 39% margin. Switching to Etsy with listing renewals, transaction fee, and domestic processing might yield a different net even when headline percentages look smaller—run both tabs before you commit inventory to one channel.
