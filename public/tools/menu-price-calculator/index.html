What this tool does

The menu price calculator connects your raw plate cost to the guest-facing price while respecting the margin you need to survive labor, rent, and marketing swings. Enter food cost, choose whether VAT is included or added at sale, dial in an optional discount scenario, and specify your target margin or markup. The outputs translate those guardrails into a recommended selling price, profit dollars, and plain-language guidance about whether the item feels underpriced, balanced, or premium relative to your goal. It is designed for iterative menu engineering: tweak one variable—like a supplier price hike—and immediately see how much the menu board must move to hold margin steady.

Who should use it

Chef-owners, beverage directors pricing non-alcoholic programs, and franchise business coaches all need quick pricing math without breaking Excel. Fast-casual brands launching LTOs, bakeries selling by weight, and takeaway shops balancing delivery discounts will return to the page whenever commodity markets spike. Hotel F&B teams juggling inclusive VAT regimes and US-style add-on tax can both map their scenario with the inclusive/exclusive toggles. Anyone who already trusts the food cost calculator should run its output straight into this tool for a full cost-to-menu story.

How to use it

Start from an honest food cost for one portion—include sauce and garnish if they belong to prime cost. Set your target margin percentage or equivalent markup depending on how your team thinks about pricing. If your market quotes VAT-inclusive prices, switch the tax mode so the calculator does not double-count. Add an expected discount only when promos are structural (happy hour, third-party app coupons). Calculate and read selling price, profit per item, and the recommendation banner. If the price feels unrealistic for your neighborhood, adjust margin temporarily to see the trade-off, then revisit operations costs or portion sizes instead of silently eating the gap.

Example

A bowl costs $4.40 fully loaded, you want 65% gross margin excluding VAT, and you plan a standing 10% loyalty discount. The calculator backs into the pre-discount list price that still yields your margin after the promo fires—often higher than intuition suggests. If VAT is 5% inclusive, toggling modes shows whether your printed menu should read $14.5 or $15.2 to guests. Document the final numbers beside each SKU so when chicken prices jump $0.40, you can rerun the tool and know exactly how much to nudge the POS.

Menu Pricing Calculator FAQ

What is a menu pricing calculator?

A menu pricing calculator helps calculate the selling price of a menu item based on food cost and profit margin.

How do restaurants calculate menu price?

Restaurants calculate menu price by dividing food cost by target margin to determine the correct selling price.

Can I include VAT and discounts?

Yes. This calculator allows you to include VAT and discounts when calculating final menu price.
