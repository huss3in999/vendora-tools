Guide: BMI Calculator

What it does

Estimates body mass index (BMI), a common screening ratio of weight to height used in public health and fitness contexts.

How to use it

Enter your weight in kilograms and height in meters, then run the calculation. Adjust inputs anytime; results update on change.

Formula

BMI = weight (kg) ÷ height (m)².

Example

70 kg and 1.75 m → BMI = 70 ÷ (1.75 × 1.75) ≈ 22.9, typically labeled “normal” on standard adult cutoffs.

FAQs

Is BMI accurate for everyone?

BMI is a population screening tool. It may misclassify very muscular people or miss risk in some older adults. Use a clinician for personal health decisions.

Why meters and kilograms?

This calculator uses metric units for consistency with the standard BMI formula. Convert imperial units before entering if needed.

Does this tool store my data?

No. Runs in your browser session; nothing is sent to a server for calculation.

What are typical BMI categories?

Common adult cutoffs: under 18.5 underweight; 18.5–24.9 normal; 25–29.9 overweight; 30+ obese (varies by guideline).
