Guide: Inflation Calculator

What it does

Applies compound inflation or deflation to nominal dollars.

How to use it

Enter amount, inflation rate, and years forward or backward.

Formula

Future ≈ Present × (1 + π)^n for constant inflation rate π.

Example

$100 at 3% inflation for 10 years ≈ $134 in equivalent nominal terms.

FAQs

Is real inflation constant?

No. This uses a single rate for illustration; CPI series differ by category.

Nominal vs real returns?

Subtract inflation from nominal return for a rough real rate.

Does this predict housing prices?

No—asset classes move differently from broad CPI.
