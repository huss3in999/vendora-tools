Guide: Simple Interest Calculator

What it does

Computes interest as principal × rate × time without compounding.

How to use it

Enter principal, annual rate, and time in years, then calculate.

Formula

Interest = P × r × t; total = P + interest.

Example

$5,000 at 4% simple interest for 3 years → $600 interest, $5,600 total.

FAQs

When is simple interest used?

Some short-term notes and teaching examples; most savings products compound.

How is time entered?

Use years as a decimal (e.g., 18 months = 1.5 years) unless the form specifies otherwise.

Can interest accrue daily on a simple-interest loan?

Some products accrue on a daily basis but still use simple interest rules—follow your promissory note.
