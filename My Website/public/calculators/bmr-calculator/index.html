Guide: BMR Calculator

What it does

Approximates calories your body may burn at rest per day based on anthropometrics and sex, using the Mifflin–St Jeor equation.

How to use it

Fill in age, height, weight, and sex, then calculate. The result is an estimate in kcal/day at rest.

Formula

Men: BMR = 10×weight(kg) + 6.25×height(cm) − 5×age + 5. Women: 10×W + 6.25×H − 5×age − 161.

Example

A 30-year-old, 175 cm, 70 kg male → ~1,665 kcal/day BMR (rounded), before activity.

FAQs

Is BMR the same as my calorie target?

No. Total daily energy needs add activity (TDEE). BMR is only the resting component.

Why might my real metabolism differ?

Equations are averages. Medications, thyroid, lean mass, and genetics can shift actual needs.

Which units does this calculator expect?

Metric inputs as labeled in the form (kg, cm, years).
